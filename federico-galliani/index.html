The instructor

Federico Galliani

Federico Galliani — known as "Portrait KID" — is an Italian black & grey portrait-realism tattoo artist and co-owner of White Label Tattoo. He teaches the Portrait Realism Academy in Milwaukee, October 6–8, 2026.

Background

Federico has been tattooing for twelve years. He started in a studio in Seregno, in the Milan area — a six-month apprenticeship, after which he was selected among the studio's best interns and kept on for over two and a half years. He worked across several studios before opening his own in 2019, and today, for seven years, he has been co-owner and resident artist at White Label Tattoo.

Style & method

His work is built on realistic black & grey portraits using true grey wash — grey made from black ink diluted with water, not grey ink — with an obsessive attention to detail. His defining principle: a portrait is judged not by how it looks the day it's done, but by how it holds and ages in the skin. Longevity is central to everything he tattoos, and it is the core of what he teaches.

Awards & conventions

  • Award-winning on the U.S. convention circuit, with multiple first-place awards — including Baltimore and Chicago (a Female XL, a portrait of Sub-Zero from Mortal Kombat on a client's thigh).
  • Awards in Italy at Milan and Bergamo.
  • Roughly twenty U.S. conventions: Milwaukee, Chicago, Baltimore, Kansas City, Minneapolis, New York and more. In Europe: Brussels and Bern. In Italy: Milan, one of the world's biggest.

Press & recognition
